matcha cinnamon rolls

inspired by broma bakery

ingredients - yields 12 rolls

​

dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp.) instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 1 egg, room temperature

cinnamon filling

  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 2/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon

matcha frosting

  • 4 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup salted butter, room temperature
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sifted matcha
  • splash of milk
  • splash of vanilla extract

instructions

  1. In a large bowl mix 2 cups of the flour, yeast, salt and sugar in a bowl.
  2. In a microwave safe bowl, microwave the milk and butter for 30 seconds until the butter is mostly melted. The mixture should be warm, but not hot. If it is too hot, make sure to let it cool before adding to the yeast to prevent killing the yeast. Add the warm milk and butter to the flour mixture.
  3. Add the egg.
  4. Beat on low speed, gradually increasing to high until completely combined, about 2 minutes.
  5. Scrape down the bowl and add 1 more cup of flour, beating until combined. Add the remaining cup of flour in a little at a time until the dough starts to pull away from the sides of the bowl and form a ball.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a well floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and you can stretch a piece of dough out between your fingers without it breaking.
  7. Transfer the dough to an oiled bowl and cover. Let rise in a warm place until the dough has doubled in size.
  8. Make the filling. Combine the sugar and cinnamon together in a small bowl.
  9. Once the dough has risen, roll it out into a long rectangle about 18 x 12 inches on a well floured surface.
  10. Spread the butter for the filling all the way to the edge of the dough. Sprinkle the sugar mixture over the butter, pressing it down to stick to the butter.
  11. From the long end, roll the dough away from you into a tight roll, sealing the bottom edge down by pinching the dough together.
  12. Use floss or a very sharp knife to cut the dough into 12 even rolls. Place the rolls in a 9 x 13 inch pan lined with parchment paper. Cover loosely and allow to rise until doubled in size and puffed up.
  13. Bake the cinnamon rolls at 350°F for 30 minutes or until they are golden brown and no longer doughy. Allow to cool.
  14. While the rolls cool, make the cream cheese frosting. In a stand mixer fit with the whisk attachment combine the cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, milk, matcha, and vanilla extract. Beat on low speed, gradually increasing to high until light and fluffy. Spread the icing evenly over the 12 rolls. Enjoy warm!
